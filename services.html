Service 01

FDAS — Fire Detection
& Alarm System

An automatic system that detects early signs of fire, alerts occupants, and notifies the fire brigade — before it's too late.

NFPA 72 RA 9514 2019 Revised IRR BFP Enforced
Key System Requirements

Automatic Detection

Smoke & heat detectors throughout the building

Audible & Visible Alarms

Horns, strobes & notification appliances

Manual Pull Stations

Strategically placed break-glass call points

Fire Alarm Control Panel

Central FACP with zone supervision

Emergency Notification

Automatic alert to fire responders

Suppression Integration

Ties into sprinkler & suppression systems

Buildings Required to Have FDAS

Hotels Dormitories Hospitals Schools Condominiums Malls Warehouses Factories High-rise Buildings Assembly Occupancies

When BFP Enforces FDAS

  • Building plan review
  • Occupancy permit processing
  • FSIC issuance
  • Annual fire inspections

Qualified Installers

  • PCAB-licensed contractor
  • Professional Electrical Engineer (PEE)
  • Professional Electronics Engineer (PECE)
  • Registered Electrical Engineer (REE)
  • NFPA 72-trained technicians

Automatic Sprinkler
System

Required in buildings 15m or higher. Designed and installed per NFPA 13 — hydraulically calculated for your specific occupancy and hazard level.

NFPA 13 NFPA 20 NFPA 25 RA 9514
Buildings Required to Have Sprinklers
High-rise Buildings Warehouses & Factories Hotels & Dormitories Hospitals Malls & Commercial Centers Large Business Occupancies Buildings 15m or Higher Hazardous Operations

What a Contractor Needs

  • Valid PCAB license — Fire Protection or Mechanical specialty
  • Professional Mechanical Engineer (PME) on staff
  • Plans signed & sealed by a licensed PME
  • Full compliance with NFPA 13 and RA 9514

Important

  • BFP does NOT install sprinklers — they inspect compliance only

Without PCAB — Consequences

  • Illegal contracting under RA 4566 / RA 11711
  • Administrative fines & penalties
  • Project stoppage
  • BFP inspection failure
  • Occupancy permit problems
  • Liability if system failure causes death
  • Contract or insurance invalidation

"Even if technically skilled — lack of PCAB license is still a legal violation."

— RA 4566 (Contractor's License Law), as amended by RA 11711
Service 03

Kitchen Fire Suppression
System (AKFSS)

Required for all commercial kitchens with grease-producing equipment. Wet chemical systems designed per NFPA 96 & 17A to stop kitchen fires instantly.

NFPA 96 NFPA 17A RA 9514 Grease Fire Protection

"Cooking equipment shall be protected by automatic kitchen hood fire suppression system in accordance with NFPA 96 and NFPA 17A."

— Fire Code of the Philippines, 2019 Revised IRR

Required For These Kitchens

Restaurants Catering Kitchens Hotels Commissaries Fast Food Chains Food Courts Commercial Cooking Areas

Required System Components

  • Kitchen hood and duct system
  • Wet chemical suppression system
  • Automatic detection
  • Manual pull station
  • Gas / electric shutoff interlock
  • Alarm integration
  • Exhaust and fresh air control
  • Class K fire extinguisher nearby
